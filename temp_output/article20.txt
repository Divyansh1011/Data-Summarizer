Half-Life 2 sweeps Bafta awards  PC first person shooter Half-Life 2 has won six Bafta Awards, including best game and best online game.The game also won Baftas for best action adventure, best PC game, art direction and animation."These awards reflect the enormous achievements, progress and diversity that we have seen in that time".Bafta said the "significant feature" of this year's awards was the number of "non-traditional games".The originality award was won by PlayStation 2 title Singstar while the children's award went to GameCube bongo rhythm game Donkey Konga.The Handheld Award went to Colin McRae Rally 2005 while the mobile category was won by Blue Tooth Byplanes.The audio award was won by Call of Duty: Finest Hour and Hitman: Contracts won the music award.
